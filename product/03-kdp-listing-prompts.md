# Module 3: KDP Listing Prompt Pack

## 25 AI Prompts for Your Amazon Listing

Paste your manuscript details into any free AI tool (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) and generate optimized listing content.

---

### Blurb Generation (5 prompts)

**Prompt 1:** "You are a copywriter for [genre] bestsellers. Here are 3 blurbs that sell well in my genre: [paste examples]. Using this structure — hook line, stakes paragraph, tease question — write 5 blurb variants for my book: [paste book details: title, protagonist, want, obstacle, stakes, genre]."

**Prompt 2:** "Rank these 5 variants by scroll-stopping power for a tired reader browsing on their phone. Be brutally honest. Then merge the best elements into one final version."

**Prompt 3:** "Write a 50-word 'hook only' version of the blurb for social media snippets."

**Prompt 4:** "Make the blurb more emotional. Add sensory language and stakes that make the reader feel the tension."

**Prompt 5:** "Shorten the blurb to exactly 100 words while keeping every element: hook, stakes, tease."

---

### Backend Keywords (5 prompts)

**Prompt 6:** "Generate 7 KDP backend keyword phrases for a [genre] book about [topic]. These should be phrases real readers type into Amazon search, not generic adjectives."

**Prompt 7:** "For each keyword phrase, tell me the search intent: 'problem-solving,' 'trope-seeking,' 'author-looking,' or 'browsing.' Rank by competitiveness."

**Prompt 8:** "Find 5 long-tail keyword opportunities (3–5 word phrases) that have lower competition but clear buyer intent."

**Prompt 9:** "Group these keywords into buckets by reader type. Which ones overlap with Amazon's auto-category suggestions?"

**Prompt 10:** "Create a keyword strategy: which 7 go into KDP backend, which 3 go into the subtitle, which 2 go into the blurb body?"

---

### Category Selection (3 prompts)

**Prompt 11:** "Given a [genre] book about [topic], which Amazon categories have the most discoverable sweet spot: high enough sales rank to matter, low enough competition to rank?"

**Prompt 12:** "For each recommended category, estimate: typical #1 rank sales, typical #100 rank sales, and how many reviews the top 20 books in that category have."

**Prompt 13:** "Which niche subcategories in [genre] are underserved right now? Suggest 3 where a new release could rank in the top 20 with under 10 reviews."

---

### Editorial Reviews (5 prompts)

**Prompt 14:** "Based on my blurb [paste], write 3 editorial-style review pulls (2–3 sentences each) that sound like a professional book reviewer."

**Prompt 15:** "Write a 'for fans of X, Y, and Z' comparison paragraph for the top of my book description."

**Prompt 16:** "Turn the best 3 reader reactions from my ARC team [paste quotes] into editorial-style pull quotes."

**Prompt 17:** "Write 5 'if you liked X, you'll love this' micro-reviews using specific comp titles in my genre."

**Prompt 18:** "Create an author bio (100 words) that establishes credibility without sounding salesy."

---

### A+ Content (5 prompts)

**Prompt 19:** "Write A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content) for my KDP listing: 5 modules with headers, body text, and image suggestions."

**Prompt 20:** "For each A+ module, write a 'customer pain → solution' narrative that addresses what the reader is looking for."

**Prompt 21:** "Create a comparison chart module: 'What you get with [Book Title] vs. reading free content or other books in the genre.'"

**Prompt 22:** "Write a 'meet the author' A+ module with a friendly, approachable tone. Include what makes my background relevant."

**Prompt 23:** "Create an A+ module that teases the next book in the series to capture pre-orders."

**Prompt 24:** "Write a 'frequently bought together' suggestion blurb for my book and a companion title."

**Prompt 25:** "Review the full listing (blurb + keywords + categories + A+ content) and suggest 3 optimizations for conversion rate."
