# Competitor Analysis — LaunchShelf

## The landscape

Authors solving "how do I launch?" today choose between four things:

| Competitor type | Example | Price | Weakness we exploit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Premium launch packages on Gumroad | "Publish & Launch Your Book on the Side" (4-in-1) | $110+ | 2× our price; general publishing focus, launch is one module |
| Courses | Self-Publishing School, SPS-style programs | $500–2,500 | Massive price gap; hours of video vs. our "open and execute" format |
| Books | *Newsletter Ninja*, *Launch to Market* etc. | $5–15 | Knowledge, not tools — no templates, no prompts, still 40 hrs of DIY |
| Free content | YouTube, r/selfpublish, blogs | $0 | Unordered and contradictory; our value IS the sequencing |
| Software | StoryOrigin ($100/yr), BookSirens, Publisher Rocket ($97+) | $50–150/yr | Tools without a plan; we're complementary and recommend them |

## Positioning

**"The $49 execution layer between free chaos and $2,000 courses."**

We don't compete on information (free everywhere) or on software (others do it better). We compete on **sequencing + done-for-you assets**: the plan in order, the emails pre-written, the prompts pre-engineered. Buyer spends time executing, not synthesizing.

## Differentiators

1. **AI-native.** The KDP Prompt Pack assumes the buyer uses free AI tools — most incumbent products predate AI or ignore it. In 2026 this is the single strongest hook in author communities.
2. **Zero-audience-first.** Nearly every competitor assumes an existing email list. Our plan starts at literally zero followers — which is the actual situation of the biggest buyer segment (first-time authors).
3. **Price/format wedge.** $49 one-time, text-based, reusable for every book. Under the impulse-buy ceiling; no video padding.
4. **Both fiction and nonfiction variants** — most competitors pick one.

## What competitors do better (be honest)

- Courses offer community & accountability — we don't. (Future: a $10/mo Discord could close this.)
- Publisher Rocket has real keyword *data*; our prompts generate candidates, not search volumes. We say so and link to Rocket as an optional upgrade (potential affiliate revenue).
- Established players have hundreds of reviews. Our first month is a credibility grind — hence the free-copy review seeding in week 1.

## Moat reality check

There is no moat — anyone can copy a template pack. Defensibility comes from: accumulating Gumroad ratings (non-transferable), the email list, affiliate relationships, and shipping updates faster than copycats. This is a cash-flow business, not a venture business, and that's fine at this price point.
